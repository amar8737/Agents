## 1. Executive Summary
- **Magnitude of the challenge:** Climate change is projected to reduce global average crop yields by **3–12 % by 2050** under a +2 °C warming scenario, and **up to 25 % under a +4 °C scenario** (IPCC AR6, 2021).  
- **Geographic disparity:** Low‑latitude, rain‑fed systems (e.g., Sub‑Saharan Africa, South Asia) face the steepest yield declines, while some high‑latitude regions may experience modest gains due to longer growing seasons.  
- **Key stressors:** Rising temperatures, altered precipitation patterns, increased frequency of heatwaves, droughts, floods, and storms, plus the spread of pests and diseases.  
- **CO₂ fertilisation:** Elevated CO₂ can partially offset yield losses for C₃ crops (wheat, rice, soy) but is limited by nutrient and water constraints.  
- **Food security risk:** By 2030, an estimated **140 million people** could be pushed into chronic food insecurity directly attributable to climate‑driven agricultural losses.  
- **Adaptation urgency:** Immediate implementation of climate‑smart agriculture (CSA) practices could **recover 30–50 %** of projected yield losses.  
- **Mitigation potential:** The agriculture sector can contribute **~11 %** of global net‑zero pathways through soil carbon sequestration, reduced methane emissions, and sustainable land‑use management.

### Key Take‑aways  

1. **Temperature & heat stress** remain the dominant yield‑reducing factor, especially for wheat, maize, and rice in tropical and subtropical zones.  
2. **Precipitation volatility** now explains ~40 % of yield variability in rain‑fed systems (World Bank 2023).  
3. **CO₂ fertilisation** can deliver a 3‑7 % yield boost for C₃ crops under optimal water and nutrient conditions, but the benefit disappears when water stress exceeds a 30 % deficit.  
4. **Pest & disease pressure** has risen 12 % globally since 2018, with *Striga* in Africa and *Brown planthopper* in Asia expanding northward (CABI 2025).  
5. **Four “hot‑spot” vulnerability clusters**: Sahel, Indo‑Gangetic Plain, Central American highlands, and the Australian wheat belt.
